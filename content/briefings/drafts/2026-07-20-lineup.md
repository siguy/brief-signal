## Proposed Lineup — Edition #23

**The Big Picture (exactly 2-3 stories, lead first):**
1.  **Inkling & the US Open-Weights Surge: Sovereignty and the Shifting Economics of AI**
    *   event: Thinking Machines (Mira Murati) released Inkling, a 975B-parameter, Apache 2.0 US-trained open model for fine-tuning on its Tinker platform.
    *   gravity: 2 distinct KBs (Bookmarks, Podcasts) × 6 distinct shows/authors (Thinking Machines, Aakash Gupta, Sriram Krishnan, Maor Shlomo, Gavin Baker, Jason Calacanis, plus analysis from AI Daily Brief).
    *   changed this week: A new, major US open-source contender with a novel business model ("model is the free sample, platform is the product") that explicitly aims to break dependence on foreign open weights and reframe value around infrastructure and fine-tuning.
    *   merges: Thinking Machines Inkling announcement, Aakash Gupta's analysis of Inkling's business model, Sriram Krishnan on the open-weights surge, Maor Shlomo on AGI likely being open, Gavin Baker on margin migration to infra, Jason Calacanis on open-source compounding for AGI.
    *   seller play: Reinforce optionality, data control, and the shift of value to infrastructure and fine-tuning platforms.

2.  **The New Iron Curtain: China's AI Policy & the Data Sovereignty Battle**
    *   event: China is reportedly exploring blocking the overseas distribution of its leading AI models, signaling a shift in its open-source policy.
    *   gravity: 2 distinct KBs (Bookmarks, Podcasts) × 5 distinct shows/authors (Reuters, Deirdre Bosa, Ethan Mollick, Rui Ma, Alibaba memo, Ali Ghodsi, Satya Nadella).
    *   changed this week: A geopolitical policy shift that fundamentally alters the global AI supply chain for open-source models, driving enterprises to reconsider data control, trusted model sources, and local deployment options.
    *   merges: China exploring blocking open-source models (AI Daily Brief 07-08), Alibaba banning Claude due to security risks (AI Daily Brief 07-06), Microsoft Frontier Tuning as a competitive response for data control (AI Daily Brief 07-08), Ali Ghodsi on context/data as the bottleneck to enterprise AI value (Stanford Playlist), Satya Nadella on Azure gaining customers from other clouds due to OpenAI exclusivity (BG2 Pod 10-31).
    *   seller play: Focus on secure, compliant Western-built models (like Gemma), trusted infrastructure for data sovereignty, and the strategic value of multi-model optionality to mitigate geopolitical risk.

3.  **The Agentic Enterprise: Uber's Blueprint for Workflow Automation & the New Economics of SaaS**
    *   event: Uber scaled its "Agentic Pods" program across 16 different business functions (e.g., Finance, Legal, Marketing), showing massive ROI in a repeatable enterprise template.
    *   gravity: 2 distinct KBs (Bookmarks, Podcasts) × 6 distinct shows/authors (Uber, Harry Stebbings, a16z, YC Head of Design, Practical AI, Replit).
    *   changed this week: Uber provides a concrete, large-scale enterprise adoption template for agentic AI, demonstrating significant ROI and driving the "SaaS-to-agents flip" with repeatable workflow automation, backed by insights into builder patterns and cost implications.
    *   merges: Uber's Agentic Pods, Harry Stebbings on replacing Salesforce with a "vibe-coded" CRM, a16z on "wasted tokens are the new headcount bloat," YC Head of Design on "one-shot tooling" and "send to an agent" features, Practical AI on building durable AI agents, and Replit's "Self-Driving Company" (from Edition #22, but new context from Uber's scaling).
    *   seller play: Frame as enabling agentic workflows, data-driven automation, and cost-efficiency through managed AI services and flexible infrastructure.

**Why the lead beats the runner-up:** Inkling's release is a concrete, US-based event that directly compounds on the open-weights/sovereignty narrative of the previous edition, with new economic and business model implications for the market that sets an immediate agenda for founders and competitive labs. China's policy shift is also critical, but Inkling provides a positive, actionable counter-narrative for reps that is more immediately "newsworthy" from a market participant perspective.
