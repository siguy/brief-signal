## The Big Picture: Compute Scarcity & The New Sovereignty Landscape

### Inkling: An Open-Weight US Model Challenges Frontier Dominance

![inkling-open-weights-sovereignty](./images/inkling-open-weights-sovereignty.jpg)

Thinking Machines, co-founded by Mira Murati, has released **Inkling**, a 975B-parameter multimodal model (41B active) under Apache 2.0, with full weights available for fine-tuning on their Tinker platform. While candidly admitting Inkling isn't the strongest overall model, commentators argue this admission *is* the business model: the model is the free sample, the fine-tuning platform is the product [Aakash Gupta on X (2 min read)](https://x.com/aakashgupta/status/2077715871221580056). This move provides a US-trained open base, breaking the two-year dependence on Chinese open weights (DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM) for American enterprises seeking to own and customize their AI stack [Thinking Machines (13 min read)](https://x.com/thinkymachines/status/2077454609551921208). The launch underscores a broader "open weights surge," driven by enterprise demand for control over data, a desire to avoid future competition from frontier labs, and geopolitical concerns [Sriram Krishnan on X (1 min read)](https://x.com/sriramk/status/2077566845431779766). This is creating a "margin migration" where value shifts from the model layer to infrastructure, as cheaper, efficient models like Inkling improve the ROI of AI spend [Gavin Baker on X (2 min read)](https://x.com/GavinSBaker/status/2076369936251851091).

**Your angle with founders:**
1.  **Where it hurts:** "What's your long-term strategy for owning your model and data stack, especially as open-weight models become genuinely competitive for your core use cases?"
2.  **How they're hedging:** "Are you evaluating solutions that let you fine-tune and control a US-based open model on your proprietary data, or are you still relying on closed models with potential future vendor lock-in?"
3.  **Where the GCP opportunity is:** Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform (GEAP) with its Model Garden provides a neutral home for both Google's Gemini models and open-source alternatives, offering the flexibility to fine-tune and deploy models with full control over your data. Agentic Data Cloud offers the data governance and tooling to own your intellectual property.

### Data Center Gridlock & Compute Futures: A Multi-Trillion Dollar Bottleneck

![data-center-gridlock-compute-futures.jpg](./images/data-center-gridlock-compute-futures.jpg)

The AI buildout faces severe physical constraints, with estimates suggesting **50% of announced AI data centers will never be built or will be significantly delayed** due to soaring costs, labor shortages, and regulatory hurdles [Anisa on Big Technology Podcast (21 min watch, 0:00:23)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1_VDhzZqbEk). OpenAI's Compute Chief, Sachin Katti, confirmed that demand "far outstrips compute supply today" and their "biggest worry" is not being able to build fast enough, citing bottlenecks across permitting, gas turbines, transformers, and skilled labor [Sachin Katti on The MAD Podcast (45 min watch, 0:00-0:19, 0:34:36)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wEZBlmvxx4o). This looming deficit is leading to **compute becoming a traded commodity**: Kalshi has launched GPU compute forward curves for Nvidia B200, H200, and A100 chips, allowing mature commodity markets to form expectations and manage risk [Tarek Mansour on X (1 min read)](https://x.com/mansourtarek_/status/2077163765020160172). With AI compute demand projected to grow to $7-10 trillion by 2030, a liquid derivative market could be 10-20x larger than the underlying spot market, mirroring "where oil was before NYMEX."

**Your angle with founders:**
1.  **Where it hurts:** "With increasing data center gridlock and compute becoming a traded commodity, what's your strategy for securing long-term, predictable compute capacity for your AI workloads?"
2.  **How they're hedging:** "Are you actively exploring different compute allocation models or partners to de-risk your growth from potential supply chain shocks and rising costs?"
3.  **Where the GCP opportunity is:** Proactive capacity planning with GPU/TPU commitments provides predictable pricing and access to secure supply. GEAP's model optionality enables efficient workload placement, allowing cheaper models for less demanding tasks. Flexible compute services like Cloud Run for GPUs can burst on demand as available, optimizing cost in a constrained market.

### Build vs. Buy Flips: Curative Cuts $600K Salesforce Contract for Internal AI

![curative-salesforce-build-vs-buy.jpg](./images/curative-salesforce-build-vs-buy.jpg)

The "build vs. buy" decision in enterprise software is flipping, with AI-native companies aggressively opting for internal development over traditional SaaS. Fred Turner, CEO of Curative, revealed his company is **cutting 80% of its SaaS spend this year**, including canceling a **$600,000 annual Salesforce contract**. Curative replaced Salesforce with a "vibe-coded" internal CRM, integrated with AI agents, that better manages their workflows and has led to engineers focusing on deployment with "Cloud Code or Codex" rather than writing new code [Fred Turner on 20VC (90 min watch, 0:47:35, 1:05:40)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GHRuerXkHlI). This internal build, running AI agents like "Gwen" for tasks like doctor credentialing (cutting costs from $50 to 20 cents per task), has seen their Anthropic spend escalate to **millions of dollars per month** [Fred Turner on 20VC (90 min watch, 0:43:00, 0:56:56)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GHRuerXkHlI). Turner states that "current gen models can do every back office task we have at Curative," shifting the focus to deployment and configuration [Fred Turner on 20VC (90 min watch, 1:22:20)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GHRuerXkHlI). This trend, also seen with Curative replacing Google's Looker with Snowflake for cost reasons, highlights a critical re-evaluation of IT budgets [Fred Turner on 20VC (90 min watch, 1:13:30)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GHRuerXkHlI).

**Your angle with founders:**
1.  **Where it hurts:** "Are you finding your existing SaaS solutions inflexible and expensive in an AI-native world, or are they enabling your teams to deeply integrate agents into core workflows?"
2.  **How they're hedging:** "What are the hidden costs of *not* owning your core workflows and data layer, especially as AI agents can now replace legacy software at a fraction of the cost?"
3.  **Where the GCP opportunity is:** Agentic Data Cloud unifies and governs your proprietary data, enabling internal AI agent development. Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform (GEAP) offers a flexible platform to build custom solutions and deploy agents, integrating with your existing systems and allowing you to "vibe-code" critical functions without SaaS lock-in.

## Builder's Corner: AI-Native Design & Agent Experience

### YC's Head of Design on AI-Native Workflows: Context, One-Shots & Machine Views

![yc-design-ai-workflows.jpg](./images/yc-design-ai-workflows.jpg)

YC's Head of Design, Ev Bufar, demonstrates how AI agents are transforming design and development, emphasizing that the bottleneck is now "creativity and imagination." Key patterns include: **Context files as the source of truth**, where every stakeholder meeting is recorded and fed into a `soul.md` file for the agent to use as an "exhaustive glossary"; **disposable/one-shot tooling**, building throwaway modals to fine-tune details and discarding them; and **designing for agents as users** by creating separate "human vs. machine" web pages. Her team built **16 different website versions** from a Pinterest mood board in one-shot, and even designed a bug/feature form whose submit button reads "send to an agent," firing off a PR directly [Ev Bufar on Y Combinator (31 min watch, 0:14:07, 0:05:46, 0:07:45)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VbqaL_eHhKY). This "just make things consistent and use coding agents for absolutely everything" approach highlights a future where users directly shape product direction.

**Why founders care:** Rapidly iterate on designs, drastically cut development cycles, ensure consistent branding with AI, and enable users to directly influence product through agentic feedback loops.

## Founder Watch

### Replit's "Self-Driving Company": Tripling Code Output with Agentic OS

Replit CEO Amjad Masad is pioneering the "self-driving company" model, demonstrating how AI agents can fundamentally reshape internal operations beyond individual productivity. In the past six months, Replit engineers have nearly **tripled code output (5.8x increase in lines of code)**, with review times, reversions, and product incidents remaining flat, and releases accelerating [Amjad Massad on AI Daily Brief (26 min watch, 02:50)](https://podcasters.spotify.com/pod/show/nlw/episodes/The-Self-Driving-Company-e3m91l5). Their agents now investigate production incidents, review pull requests, triage support tickets, and even analyze business data. A key unlock: giving agents access to *all* existing systems, including **GitHub, GCP, Linear, Notion, Slack, and Zendesk**, creating a "leap forward in productivity" by providing full context across the organization [Amjad Massad on AI Daily Brief (26 min watch, 05:40)](https://podcasters.spotify.com/pod/show/nlw/episodes/The-Self-Driving-Company-e3m91l5). Replit also replaced a "seven-figure SaaS solution" with an internal AI app at a fraction of the cost. The mindset shift: people don't feel automated, "they feel like they've been promoted" from "doers to directors" [Amjad Massad on AI Daily Brief (26 min watch, 04:47, 10:59)](https://podcasters.spotify.com/pod/show/nlw/episodes/The-Self-Driving-Company-e3m91l5).
